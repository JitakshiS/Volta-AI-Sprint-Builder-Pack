The golden rule: Lead with the problem, not the features. Your audience cares about why this matters before they care about how it works. Talk about the person you're solving for, not the tech you used.

1
The Hook
10 seconds
Imagine you're explaining your app to a friend at a coffee shop. One sentence. What would you say?

Avoid: "I built a booking app with React and Supabase"
Better: "I built a way for yoga studios to fill their classes without the DM chaos"

2
The Problem
10 seconds
What frustration does your user deal with right now? How does your app change that?
3
The Walkthrough
90 seconds
Describe what your user does, not what your app has. Instead of "my app has a booking form," say "a client picks a class and books it in 20 seconds."

How to practice:

1. Read your script out loud, not in your head. It sounds different when you say it.

2. Open your app and practice the walkthrough while talking. You need to do both at once.

3. Time yourself. If you're over 2 minutes, shorten the walkthrough, not the problem or intro.

4. Do it twice. The second time is always better.
